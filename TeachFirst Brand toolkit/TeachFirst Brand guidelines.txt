    <p class="section-tag">Motion &amp; Interaction</p>
    <h2>Animation principles</h2>
    <p>Motion should feel purposeful and efficient. The brand is confident and energetic — not fussy. Transitions are quick, reveals are clean.</p>

    <div class="motion-note">
      <strong style="font-size:0.88rem; color:var(--tf-grey-800);">Core rules:</strong>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Duration:</strong> Interactive transitions 150–200ms. Page-level reveals 300–400ms.</li>
        <li><strong>Easing:</strong> <span class="inline-code">ease</span> or <span class="inline-code">cubic-bezier(0.25, 0, 0.1, 1)</span> for most transitions. Avoid bounce/spring unless the product is explicitly playful.</li>
        <li><strong>Hover states:</strong> All interactive elements must have a visible hover. Colour shift or subtle shadow lift. Never opacity-only.</li>
        <li><strong>Page load:</strong> A single fade-up stagger on key sections is enough. Don't animate everything — it dilutes impact.</li>
        <li><strong>Respect user preference:</strong> Wrap animation in <span class="inline-code">@media (prefers-reduced-motion: no-preference)</span>.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="code-block"><span class="comment">/* Standard hover transition — apply to all interactive elements */</span>

    <h2>How we write in digital products</h2>
    <p>Teach First's brand voice is positive, purposeful, and empowering. In a digital product context that means: short sentences, plain language, and a warm but direct tone. Write like a knowledgeable colleague, not a policy document.</p>

    <div class="do-dont">

          <li>"You're all set — we'll send a confirmation shortly."</li>
          <li>"Can't find what you're looking for? Contact your DL."</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="dont-box">
        <p class="label">✗ Avoid this</p>
        <ul>
          <li>"Please be advised that your scheduled call window will commence on…"</li>
          <li>"An error has been encountered. Please try again."</li>
          <li>"Your submission has been successfully processed."</li>
          <li>"Leverage the resources available via the portal."</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

    <h3>Microcopy principles</h3>
    <table>
      <tr><th>Context</th><th>Approach</th></tr>
      <tr><td>Empty states</td><td>Warm and action-oriented. "Nothing here yet — your calls will appear once scheduled."</td></tr>
      <tr><td>Error messages</td><td>Say what happened and what to do next. No blame, no jargon.</td></tr>
      <tr><td>Success messages</td><td>Brief and positive. Confirm what happened, not what the system did.</td></tr>
      <tr><td>Form labels</td><td>Short noun phrases. Not "Please enter your…" — just "Email address".</td></tr>
      <tr><td>CTA buttons</td><td>Verb-first. "Book a call", "View your programme", "Download guide". Avoid "Submit" or "Click here".</td></tr>

    </table>
